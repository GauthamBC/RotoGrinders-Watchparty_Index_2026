The best U.S. cities for 2026 World Cup watch parties

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is already underway across North America, and fans do not need a match ticket to be part of the atmosphere.

With games being played across the U.S., Canada and Mexico, supporters are gathering in bars, fan zones and local watch parties throughout the tournament. But which U.S. cities are best set up for fans watching away from the stadium?

To find out, RotoGrinders ranked 343 U.S. cities with populations above 100,000, covering major cities across the country.

Each city received a score out of 100 based on four factors: , , , and .

Key findings

Overall, RotoGrinders ranked cities by how well suited they are to host and support World Cup watch parties, using a score that combines venue access, fan demand, local event activity and practical matchday convenience.

1

San Francisco ranks No. 1 overall

San Francisco finished top of the index with an overall score of 54.5 out of 100, helped by the strongest bar access score in the top 10.

2

New York has the strongest fan demand

New York ranked second overall and recorded a fan demand score of 71.0, the highest score for that category in the top 10.

3

Atlanta leads the top 10 for watch party activity

Atlanta completes the top three overall and recorded the highest watch party activity score among the top 10, at 52.7.

4

Houston ranks highest for kickoff convenience and affordability

Houston stands out for practical matchday viewing, with a kickoff convenience and affordability score of 83.3, the highest in the top 10.

5

Dallas and Kansas City offer a strong matchday mix

Dallas and Kansas City both scored 75.0 for kickoff convenience and affordability, while also posting watch party activity scores above 50.
